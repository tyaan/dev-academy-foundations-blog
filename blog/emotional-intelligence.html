Emotional Intelligence

What is emotional intelligence? Emotional intelligence can mean many things. For me, emotional intelligence is about reading peoples expressions, deducing the emotional motivations behind others behaviour, feeling empathy, and understanding my own internal emotions and resulting behaviours. There can be a lot of overlap between each of these things. For example, reading peoples expressions is easier if you feel empathy, and your own internal emotional state matches whatever expression you see on a person in front of you, and understanding your own internal emotions and behaviours can help to deduce the emotional motivations of others behaviour.

What is the difference between EQ and IQ? EQ and IQ, meaning emotional quotient and intelligence quotient, refer to a persons level of emotional and intellectual intelligence. IQ seems to usually measured by a persons ability to solve complex problems using reason and logic and intuit underlying patterns in complex systems, whereas emotional intelligence is more defined by the empathetic skills mentioned above. There is a lot of debate around whether IQ and EQ can be accurately measured because there are so many factors. Tests would need to take into account cultural differences, and not focus too much on specific topics or types of problems that some people may be better trained in. Creating a test that generalizes to every person in the world and gives an accurate measure of IQ or EQ is very difficult, and I personally think assigning general intelligence values to people is silly, and can be dangerous if the tests are biased towards specific cultures or types of intelligence.

Why is emotional intelligence useful? Developing stronger emotional intelligence can help you gain control over your own behaviour and emotional state, improve your well-being, work better with others, form stronger emotional connections with others, and help others improve their own emotional well-being.

How to improve emotional intelligence and emotional well-being? Practices that improve emotional intelligence include meditation and various mindfulness practices, among many others. The world of emotional intelligence practices is vast and complex, with entire academic fields dedicated to understanding human emotions and psychology and developing theraputic tools and practices to improve emotional well-being. Practices like meditation are easy for anyone to do and are proven to improve well-being to some extent by relaxing the nervous system and bringing emotion and thought processes more into the concious mind. However, improving well-being by making changes to patterns of behaviour and emotions and underlying perceptions about yourself, others, and the world, generally can be a significantly more complicated task, which can require very different things for different people. In my experience, emotional well-being can be hugely improved by just getting more physical excercise, dedicating more time to things I enjoy, and making an effort to develop close friendships. Practices like meditation and mindfulness can be a good step in developing the emotional intelligence required to understand yourself, so that you can work out what it is that you really need.
