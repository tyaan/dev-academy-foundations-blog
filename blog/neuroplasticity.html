Neuroplasticity

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people.

Many people are told early on in life that they are smart or dumb, or particularly good at some things and bad at others. For example, take someone who believes they are “bad at math”. Maybe they were told that once by a teacher, or maybe a particular learning style didn’t work for them at some point, and they struggled more than other students, and internalized the belief that they just aren’t good at math.

Understanding neuroplasticity can help remind people that there is no good or bad or smart or dumb, there is just people who have built the neural pathways to do particular tasks, and people who haven’t built those pathways. When you understand this, you can shake off the belief that your abilities or intelligence is fixed, and know that all you need to do to learn something is find a learning style that works for you, and practice it consistently over time to build those neural pathways.

Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your benefit.

The principles of neuroplasticity outline a range of rules explaining what types of practice and training are most effective for learning new things. They mostly focus on the idea that repetition strengthens and maintains neural pathways, which will decay when unused. They also say that intensity matters, and you can form stronger neural pathways by practicing something at a high intensity. I have found in my own life that a good way to learn new things and develop knowledge in an area is not to learn slowly and consistently over time, but to study very intensely for short periods with longer periods in between where you continue to use the skill a moderate amount, and let the knowledge and muscle memory sink in before adding more new information. I think this is the approach I will continue to take when it comes to learning coding and other tech skills.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

Activities that increase neuroplasticity include: playing video games, learning languages, making music and traveling. Things that increase neuroplasticity in general seem to usually either involve some new type of stimulus (like traveling to new places), some type of creative problem solving (like in video games and making music), or making new connections between things (like learning languages).

Growth Mindset

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant.

A growth mindset is the idea that your goal with any activity should simply be growth, rather than any particular achievement. It can be a helpful way of thinking to reduce lack of motivation after a goal is achieved, issues with comparing yourself with others and negative self beliefs around abilities and intelligence. With a growth mindset you only ever need to compare yourself to yourself, and your goal at all times is to be a better version of yourself. A growth mindset is related to neuroplasticity in that it acknowledges that whatever you have achieved and wherever you are currently at in life, you can always grow and learn and change more, and there is no reason to assume your abilities have a ceiling, or set fixed and final life goals for anything.
