Bootcamp Learning Plan

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

I always thought that would be fun, and after talking with one of the facilitators who works in game design, it feels like it would be achievable. I think that if I build solid programming foundation during dev academy, and then spend some time learning Unity and make a small game or 2, I might be able to get a junior role as a game developer in Wellington.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

I think my biggest strength will probably be my comfortability with self-learning new tech stuff. I think I am quite used to the google/stack overflow/chat gpt method of learning at this point. This hasn't really changed since foundations, I think I have a good understanding of learning styles that work for me.
I think my biggest limitation will probably be all the non-technical writing, its just not something I'm super comfortable with. This also hasn't really changed since foundations, all of this writing about how I'm feeling does not come naturally to me.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

I think I might struggle a little bit with the pair programming depending on who I am partnered with. I like doing things by myself, so I think it might be hard to share a project with one other person.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would like to improve my social skills and ability to connect with different people. It would be nice to make some friends and practice working on projects with one or two other people and feel comfortable sharing ideas and work with them.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

I expect the bootcamp team to provide reasonably regular support when I get stuck on things. I also expect semi-regular check ins to make sure I am feeling ok about the work and not overwhelmed. I am also trusting them to ensure that I come out of bootcamp with the skills required to get a job, assuming that I am motivated to learn during bootcamp.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

I will seek help during bootcamp if I am stuck on something technical for more than 20 minutes ish. I will also seek help if I am starting to feel overwhelmed and worried that I won't be able to finish something on time.
I am committing to completely focus on dev academy from 9 - 5 every weekday during bootcamp, with 2 - 3 days per week on campus. I will also assess my workload and work on Saturdays also if I need to. I think if I consistently focus on dev academny during these hours and rest outside of these hours, I will probably be ok.

Foundations Learning Plan

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

My long term goal is to establish a stable career as either a software developer or data scientist. I would like to aim for senior roles within 5 years of starting full time work.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

Strengths: I think I have a good understanding of my learning style, as I have done quite a lot of self directed learning in tech and also music. I know that I learn best by just doing projects that require more knowledge than I have, and working things out along the way. I can also just read books/articles and retain information reasonably well but I think I’m a little on the ADHD end of things and have to focus in bursts and take breaks often, and maybe be doing something else at the same time for that to work.
Weaknesses: I have a tendency to get irritated by exercises that feel like they are a bit of a waste of time, and try to skip over them, like ones where you reflect on what you’ve learned and stuff like that. But I think in reality these are actually good for solidifying knowledge, I don’t retain stuff super well by rushing through it.

What skills (non-technical/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would like to get better at collaborating with other people without trying to control everything. I'm also hoping to develop more patience in learning, and resist the urge to want to rush through things.

An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, Facilitators and industry/community representatives

I will always try to stay aware of other people’s emotions, and ensure that I treat everyone with respect. I will always treat the facilitators with respect and try to remind myself that they are also people just trying to help and it’s not their fault if something doesn’t make sense.

What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you

When things are building up and starting to get to me, I will try and take breaks and do something else for a day or a few hours (or just however much time I can realistically take away from the course). Exercise, playing music, and walks in the bush are good things for me to do to reset.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

If I am struggling with something technical, I’ll give it at least a couple of tries myself to try and work it out, but will reach out to facilitators if it’s getting to the point where I am spending too much time trying to solve a problem on my own. I’ll try not to spend more than a few hours on anything before asking a question. If I need emotional help, I’ll probably just go to a friend outside the course, but I also might talk to other students and see if they are feeling the same way.

A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team

I expect a reasonable level of support from the facilitators to help me past anything I get stuck on. I figure there are more students than facilitators so they won’t be able to help everyone all the time, so I’ll do my best to work things out on my own first, and ask for help when I really need it.
