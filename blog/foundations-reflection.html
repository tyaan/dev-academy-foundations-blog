Foundations Reflections

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

I feel like it helped me feel more relaxed about my plan for the future. The whole growth mindset thing makes a lot of sense. Rather than stressing about achieving some specific goal, and feeling like your life won't be good until you get there, it feels a lot better to just accept wherever you are now and make your goal always to just be better.

What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

Doing a lot of writing was quite challenging, I haven't done much writing probably since high school. I did some in university, but that was mostly like lab reports. Just writing my thoughts and feelings was not something I am used to at all.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I have heard that most tech employers value your attitude as much as your technical skills. This makes sense considering that most jobs will probably have you doing specific things that you might need to learn once you start the job anyway, so they would want to just look for people that are motivated, can work in a team, and solve problems well by themselves. Emotional well-being also plays a huge part in your ability to be productive and engage your problem solving ability, so it makes sense to focus in on that.

Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time?

No it doesn't feel like a waste of time. A couple of the excercises felt like a bit too much, like almost a little bit condescending because of how they were almost treating me like a child becoming aware of emotions for the first time. But I understand why its important, and some of the excercises were actually very helpful.

Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

No I don't think so. The programming stuff has been fine, it was helpful to do the human skills along side it to get constant reminders to check in with how I am feeling and how I am approaching solving problems. I think it helped to solidify the mindset that I should be in when programming in future.
